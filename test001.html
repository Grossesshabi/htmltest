Galois Theory I

Galois theory is an interesting mathematical topic. The theory has a very elegant structure, and it is the starting point of many aspects of modern number theory. However, it looks quite formidable for most novices who are little trained in professional mathematics.

If so, Is it possible to introduce it to a wider class of audiences beyond the university students majored in mathematics?

I think the answer should be, ‘YES’, and I even believe that, it is possible to explain this beautiful theme to high school students, as the title shows.

In my opinion, although there are many standard textbooks in abstract algebra and ‘story books’ on Galois theory, these materials are either too formal or too sketchy. A concrete introduction to the whole theory without too much deep concepts is still lacking. So I just make this attempt.

Instead, we’ll start with some motivating examples which are so elementary that they frequently appear in your exercise and exams.
